Fabric Care Guide

Proper care ensures your natural fiber garments will last for years. Each fabric has unique properties and requirements—follow these guidelines to keep your pieces looking their best.

Cotton Care

Washing: Machine wash in cold to warm water with mild detergent. Turn garments inside out to preserve color and reduce pilling. Separate whites and colors to prevent color transfer.

Drying: Tumble dry on low heat or line dry to prevent shrinkage. Remove promptly to minimize wrinkles. High heat can damage cotton fibers over time.

Ironing: Iron while slightly damp on medium-high heat. Use steam for stubborn wrinkles. Cotton can withstand high temperatures, making it easy to achieve crisp results.

Storage: Fold and store in a cool, dry place. Ensure garments are completely dry before storing to prevent mildew.

Silk Care

Washing: Hand wash in cold water with pH-neutral detergent designed for silk. Never wring or twist—gently squeeze excess water. Some silk items may be dry clean only; always check the label.

Drying: Roll in a clean towel to absorb moisture, then lay flat to dry away from direct sunlight. Never tumble dry silk as heat can damage the delicate fibers.

Ironing: Use the lowest heat setting and iron on the reverse side while slightly damp. Place a pressing cloth between the iron and silk to prevent shine or scorching.

Storage: Store silk garments on padded hangers or folded with acid-free tissue paper. Keep away from direct sunlight which can fade colors. Ensure good air circulation to prevent moisture buildup.

Linen Care

Washing: Machine wash in cold to warm water. Linen can withstand regular washing and actually becomes softer over time. Use gentle cycle for delicate items.

Drying: Tumble dry on low heat or line dry. Remove while slightly damp to make ironing easier. Linen is prone to wrinkling, but this is part of its natural charm.

Ironing: Iron while damp on high heat with steam. Linen requires high temperatures to achieve smooth results. For a more relaxed look, skip ironing and embrace the natural texture.

Storage: Fold loosely or hang to allow air circulation. Linen breathes well and resists moths, making it excellent for long-term storage.

Wool Care

Washing: Hand wash in cold water with wool-specific detergent, or use the gentle/wool cycle on your machine. Avoid agitation which causes felting. Never use hot water.

Drying: Gently squeeze out excess water—never wring. Lay flat on a clean towel away from heat and direct sunlight. Reshape while damp. Never tumble dry wool.

Ironing: Use low heat with steam, placing a damp pressing cloth between the iron and wool. Most wool garments benefit from steaming rather than direct ironing.

Storage: Fold and store in breathable bags with cedar blocks or lavender sachets to deter moths. Never store wool in plastic, which can trap moisture. Clean before storage to remove oils that attract insects.

General Tips

Washing Frequency: Natural fibers don't need to be washed after every wear. Wool naturally resists odors and can be aired out between wears. Cotton and linen can typically be worn 2-3 times before washing, depending on use.

Stain Treatment: Address stains promptly but gently. Blot, don't rub. Test cleaning solutions on an inconspicuous area first. For silk and wool, consider professional cleaning for serious stains.

Seasonal Storage: Clean all garments before storing for the season. Use breathable storage bags or containers. Add cedar or lavender for moth protection in wool storage.

Professional Care: When in doubt, consult a professional cleaner, especially for structured garments like blazers and coats, or delicate pieces with special embellishments.
