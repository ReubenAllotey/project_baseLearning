Our Commitment to Sustainability

Fashion doesn't have to cost the earth. We're committed to creating beautiful garments while protecting the planet and supporting the people who make them.

Natural Fibers Only

We work exclusively with natural, biodegradable fibers. No polyester, no nylon, no acrylic. Just cotton, silk, linen, and wool—materials that return to the earth at the end of their lifecycle.

Natural fibers require less energy to produce, shed fewer microplastics, and often come from renewable sources. Our organic cotton uses 91% less water than conventional cotton and eliminates harmful pesticides from the growing process.

Ethical Production

Every mill and workshop we partner with undergoes rigorous auditing. We verify fair wages, safe working conditions, and reasonable hours. Many of our partners are family-owned businesses where craft traditions have been passed down through generations.

We believe in long-term relationships, not transactional sourcing. By working with the same artisans year after year, we ensure stability for their businesses and livelihoods.

Transparency

You have a right to know where your clothes come from. Each product page on our site includes information about the fabric's origin and the people who made it. We trace our supply chain from field to finished garment.

Durability Over Disposability

The most sustainable garment is one that lasts. We design for longevity, using time-tested construction methods and high-quality materials. Our clothes are meant to be worn for years, even decades, developing character and patina along the way.

We also offer repair services for our garments, because we believe in extending the life of every piece we create.

Minimal Waste

Our packaging is minimal and recyclable. We use FSC-certified paper, avoid plastic where possible, and encourage customers to reuse our cloth garment bags.

In production, we work with mills that repurpose textile scraps into insulation, padding, or recycled yarn. Nothing goes to landfill.

Our Goals

We're not perfect, but we're committed to continuous improvement. By 2025, we aim to:

Source 100% of our cotton from organic or regenerative farms. Achieve carbon neutrality across our entire supply chain. Transition all packaging to compostable or reusable materials. Expand our repair and recycling program to all customers worldwide.

Sustainability isn't a marketing buzzword for us—it's the foundation of everything we do.
